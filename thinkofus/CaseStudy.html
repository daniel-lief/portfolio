Think of Us Case Study

01. Info

Organization: Think of Us

Role: Frontend Developer

Dates: January 2025 - May 2025

02. Introduction

Think of Us is a non-profit organization committed to helping foster care youth across the United States. They connect youth, parents, and caregivers with external organizations that can provide direct and local support resources. They have served over 6,000 individuals in the US.

I was brought in as a frontend developer in a full-stack team, and focused on creating a new organization portal web app for them. This portal accomplishes several functions:

  • External organizations officially create an account with Think of Us
  • They get manually authorized by an administrator who reviews their history and purpose
  • They provide key information to Think of Us allowing them to be effectively connected with local people who can use their resources best
  • They log and submit transactions which represent all of the events and resources they give out, allowing Think of Us to measure their impact

My role was to design and implement three key pages to accomplish this functionality:

  • The home page, providing basic information and directing organizations to either sign in or sign up
  • The dashboard page, the page that organizations will spend most of their time on where they can view historical transactions, submit new transactions, edit their information, and update their status
  • The admin page, where official administrators can login and view a list of organizations, approve any new ones, and see all the information and full history of transactions for each organization

I collaborated very closely with the backend developers on my team to coordinate displaying data from a database. Our effective teamwork ensured a fully functional and easy to use final product.

I used Figma to design lo-fi and hi-fi wireframes. I then engineered the implementation in NextJS, HTML, Tailwind CSS, and Typescript. During the research phase, we focused on understanding the different types of users that our portal needed to support, as well as paying careful consideration to how we organized the data presented to the user, as there was a significant amount of it and several different types.

03. Research & Ideation

Because this one portal supports all use cases for Think of Us, we spent some time trying to understand the different perspectives that would be using it. I created two user personas to represent two unique conditions that we needed to design for.

2 User Personas I made.

From analyzing this information, we concluded several things that were important to the success of the portal:

  • It should immediately provide basic information on what this portal is and what Think of Us does, for first-time external organizations
  • It has to allow both organizations and individual administrators to log in
  • On the organization side, it should be very clear and easy to take the initial steps of updating information and submitting transactions
  • For administrators it should condense the information to provide an overhead view, and allow further engagement with each individual organization that needs to be managed

With that we were able to ideate and brainstorm some specific ideas for features within the portal that could satisfy those criteria and provide a pleasant user experience.

Home Page

  • Basic information about Think of Us and how to get started with the portal
  • Direct to sign in/sign up page

Sign In/Sign Up Page

  • One sign in page that works for an organizational email or an internal Think of Us email

Organization Dashboard

  • Organized into sections in a table-like format
  • Delineates four key functionalities of the portal: displaying current information, editing information, displaying historical transactions, and adding a new transaction

Admin Dashboard

  • Completely different style dashboard
  • List of organization names, along with small tooltips on what their status is (colors or icons)
  • Clicking on one of them opens a detailed view showing their information
  • They can also be approved or denied if they are requesting an account

Now that we had clear thoughts on what the portal should look like, we moved on to the design phase.

04. Designs

The flow of designs was a bit unique for this project. Because there was significant work on the backend necessary for this features to be implemented, I communicated with the backend developers on our team and together we decided it would be best to first prioritize designing and implementing the organization dashboard page, as it was the page that required the most work on both the front and back ends, and it would link well to other pages we had to implement later.

So, I started by creating a low fidelity wireframe for this dashboard page, taking the ideas from the research phase and materializing them into an effective layout and style.

Lo-fi organization dashboard.

I chose to design the delineation as a sort of grid format, with boxes representing each function. There are two distinct columns; the left focused on the organization’s profile (the information they provide to Think of Us about their organization itself), and the right focused on transactions.

At this point the transactions system was quite fluid and especially with the backend work going on concurrently, I wanted to design something simple at first that could be expanded later. Although here it only showed two values for each transaction: the date and the description, I planned for future modifications by designing it like a table, which can easily be expanded to add many more columns and values in the future.

Next, I went straight to making a high fidelity design for this dashboard page, iterating on the lo-fi version and focusing on maintaining a consistent color theme and style.

Hi-fi organization dashboard.

Here are the key elements of this design:

  • Deliberate use of colors to match Think of Us’s existing theme of aqua and white, using aqua as the primary color to highlight section headers and action buttons while using white and gray for everything else
  • Making the grid system very clear with how each box is separated but similar
  • Consistent font sizing and weights (except for the description of transactions, which we ended up changing later)

Next, I branched out to some of the other pages of the portal. I made high fidelity designs for the home page, and the admin dashboard page, focusing on keeping them simple while displaying all the features that we wanted to add based on our research.

Hi-fi home page.

Hi-fi admin dashboard.

These designs were quite simple and flexible because I knew the whole process was very dynamic, especially with backend features and such, so instead of trying to plan for every detail at this point, I wanted to lay out the groundwork and foundations for the final product. These served as a stepping stone for the most important phase of the project, developing the frontend website and connecting it to the backend.

05. Final Product

We worked together as a team to implement the final web app portal. I specifically did coding in NextJS, Typescript, HTML, and CSS to convert my designs and our ideas into a working product. As the backend team developed and made modifications, I updated the design and features visible within the portal. The end result was synthesized from all of our research, ideation, designs, and engineering we did throughout our time working on it.

Home page.

Organization dashboard.

Admin dashboard.

Admin detailed view.

Some important changes we made from the initial designs:

  • Adding a more meaningful graphic on the home page that demonstrates Think of Us’s positive impact for organizations
  • Showing pending changes to profile information on the organization dashboard helps provide clarity on what still needs to be approved manually
  • More information provided in the profile
  • Change to the transaction system allowing each organization to make their own custom fields they want to report, better fitting the target audience which could be a very broad range of organizations that do completely different things
  • Instead of trying to fit long descriptions and other fields in the small table for transactions, shows the basic information and then has a button to open up a detailed view of that specific transaction, showing all fields
  • Used clearer color indicators for the admin dashboard to allow the admin to quickly and easily figure out which organizations need attention immediately

06. Outcomes

In the end, we accomplished our overall goal of developing a new web app portal for Think of Us to use for both external organizations submitting information and data, and internal administrators for managing their partnered organizations. This portal allows organizations to navigate the signing up process, information submission, and transactions system smoothly, and administrators to do their job of approving changes efficiently.

I gained significant skills through this project. On the design side, I got more experience in utilizing user personas, ideating to come up with features, and making lo-fi and hi-fi Figma designs. For development, I learned best frontend development practices in Tailwind CSS, NextJS and Typescript, and gained experience in connecting several different aspects of the backend to the frontend.

One of the more unique things I learned was how to understand the many different perspectives using the application. This portal could be used by a single person, a small local organization, or even a large national organization, and it was crucial to design with the full audience in mind. Planning features carefully and iterating on designs to ensure the app provides the best experience possible for all use cases was a great thing to practice and gain experience in.
